First conference?

How MUN works

You'll represent a country, character, or organization in a committee, debating assigned topics while staying true to your assignment's real position.

Get your assignment

Before the conference you receive a committee and a country or character to represent, plus the topics for debate.

Research

Read the background guide and dig into your assignment's real-world stance. Know your allies and your opposition.

Write your position paper

Summarize your research and your assignment's position on the topic. Format requirements are below.

Debate & resolve

Speak, caucus, form blocs, and draft resolutions with fellow delegates — in character, start to finish.

Required for all delegates

Position papers

Length1 page
SpacingSingle-spaced
FontTimes New Roman 12
Margins1 inch
CitationsChicago style
Sources pageSeparate page

Header — top of page

  • Your name
  • Your school
  • Your committee
  • Your character or country name

Structure — three paragraphs

  • ¶ 1 — Background: the topic and its context
  • ¶ 2 — Position: where your character or country stands
  • ¶ 3 — Goals: your proposed solutions for committee

Keep it clean

No headers beyond the required one, no section breaks, and no images. The one-page limit excludes your sources page. Chicago-style citations go on that separate final page.
